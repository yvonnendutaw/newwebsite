A peer-to-peer job facilitation solution for the world’s biggest labour force


Netwookie

Netwookie is a peer-to-peer mobile application for a transitioning job market (from informal to formal) as found in major cities of the developing world. It aims at providing transparency and opportunity to marginalised people by facilitating job creation whilst leveraging the unique dynamics found in these environments. Generated data provides an unprecedented solution for companies and organisation to source qualified workers from the world’s biggest labour force and assists in designing locally adapted economic and social interventions. brFounded by Maximilian Bock in 2015, Netwookie is the recent winner of the Cambridge Enterprise Business Competition, and now operational in Cambridge and Nairobi, Kenya.
